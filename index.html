My Family


MY FAMILY

I come from a family of six, with an older brother, a little sister, and a little brother who is our bunso I’m right in the middle, and I’ve grown to embrace how this position lets me connect with each of my siblings in unique ways. I’ve always been a daddy’s girl, sharing a special bond with him that brings me so much comfort, especially since he’s been there with us in Albay while we navigate life apart from Mom. My mother works hard in Manila to support us, while my father and the rest of my family make our home in Albay. Though the distance between my mom and us can be difficult at times, we stay close through regular calls and cherish every moment we get to spend together when she comes home. Even with the challenges of living apart, my family remains the center of my world, and I’m grateful for the love and strength we share with one another.

My Friends


MY FRIENDS

This is my friends even though I’m based here in Cavite and my closest friends are all back in Albay, distance hasn’t weakened our bond one bit. We first met years ago and built a connection that goes beyond just being nearby whether it’s sharing stories about our days over long video calls, sending each other photos of what’s happening in our lives, or planning our next reunion whenever I get to visit home. They know all about my family situation, with Mom working in Manila and the rest of us split between places, so they always know how to make me feel less alone when I’m missing everyone back in Albay. We count down the days until holidays or special occasions when we can finally meet up, eat our favorite local dishes together, and pick up right where we left off like no time has passed at all.
